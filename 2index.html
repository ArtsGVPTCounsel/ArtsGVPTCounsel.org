    I. Executive Summary: The Gap Between Mandate and Implementation

    Summary of Findings: Systemic Dereliction of Duty

    This comprehensive audit confirms the critical findings that during the initial implementation phase of Proposition 28 (The Arts and Music in Schools (AMS) Funding Guarantee and Accountability Act), covering Fiscal Year (FY) 2023–24 and projecting into FY 2024–25, Local Educational Agencies (LEAs) within Humboldt County have failed demonstrably to convert guaranteed arts funding into mandated instructional employment. The assertion that there have been zero new certificated arts teachers hired by key reporting districts for FY 2023–24 is supported by official data indicating non-expenditure and programmatic delay, which collectively constitutes a systemic failure to meet the core legal objective of the Act.1

    The central issue is not merely the presence of a rural teacher shortage, but rather the documented non-compliance and procedural delay exhibited by LEAs, specifically the failure to initiate spending and secure personnel in the first year.
